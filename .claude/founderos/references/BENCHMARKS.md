## Efficiency benchmarks

### Burn multiple

Burn multiple = Net burn / Net new ARR

| Rating | Burn multiple |
|--------|-------------|
| Amazing | <1x |
| Great | 1-1.5x |
| Good | 1.5-2x |
| OK | 2-3x |
| Concerning | >3x |

### Rule of 40

Revenue growth rate + profit margin >= 40%

- Early stage: growth rate matters more, negative margins acceptable
- Growth stage: approaching profitability, should trend toward Rule of 40
- Late stage: should meet or exceed Rule of 40

### CAC payback period

| Rating | Payback |
|--------|---------|
| Excellent | <6 months |
| Good | 6-12 months |
| Acceptable | 12-18 months |
| Concerning | 18-24 months |
| Bad | >24 months |

## Runway guidelines

| Runway remaining | Action |
|-----------------|--------|
| 18+ months | Comfortable — execute the plan |
| 12-18 months | Start thinking about next raise or path to profitability |
| 6-12 months | Actively fundraise or cut burn |
| <6 months | Emergency — cut costs aggressively and/or raise bridge |
| <3 months | Crisis — drastic measures required |

---

## How to use these benchmarks

1. **Context matters** — benchmarks vary by industry, geography, and business model. Use as directional guidance, not absolute targets.
2. **Compare to yourself** — your trend matters more than absolute numbers. Improving metrics are more important than hitting benchmarks.
3. **Stage-appropriate** — don't compare seed metrics to Series B benchmarks.
4. **Founders often over-index on vanity metrics** — focus on the metrics that predict survival and growth (runway, revenue growth, retention).
