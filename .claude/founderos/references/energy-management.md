# Energy Management

Reference for founder energy, deep work, and schedule design. Load before running `/founder:energy`.

---

## The maker vs manager schedule (Paul Graham)

### Manager schedule
- Days divided into 1-hour blocks
- Meetings can go anywhere — interruptions are expected
- Context switching is normal
- Good for: status updates, check-ins, coordination

### Maker schedule
- Days divided into half-day blocks minimum
- Meetings are expensive — a single meeting can destroy a half-day
- Deep focus is the currency
- Good for: strategy, writing, product thinking, decision analysis

**The founder's challenge:** You need both. The solution is to batch them, not mix them.

## Recommended weekly structure

| Day | Morning (9-12) | Afternoon (1-5) | Type |
|-----|----------------|-----------------|------|
| Monday | Deep work | Internal meetings | Mixed |
| Tuesday | Deep work | External meetings | Mixed |
| Wednesday | Deep work | Strategy / thinking | Full maker |
| Thursday | Team meetings | External meetings | Full manager |
| Friday | Weekly review | Flex / overflow | Review |

**Key principles:**
- Morning = deep work (highest cognitive energy for most people)
- Afternoon = meetings and reactive work
- One full maker day per week (Wednesday) — no meetings, no exceptions
- Friday afternoon = flex time for overflow, or end early if caught up

## Deep work protocol

**Minimum:** 3 blocks per week, 3 hours each = 9 hours of deep work

**Rules during deep work:**
1. No Slack, no email, no notifications
2. Phone on Do Not Disturb
3. Single task per block — decide before the block starts
4. No meetings, no "quick calls"
5. Tell the team when you're in deep work so they don't interrupt

**Best uses for deep work:**
- Strategic planning and thinking
- Writing (board updates, investor updates, blog posts)
- Product direction and roadmap decisions
- Financial modeling and analysis
- Hiring decisions (reviewing candidates, structuring roles)
- Learning (reading, research, skill building)

## Meeting hygiene

### Default meeting lengths
- 25 minutes (not 30) — leaves 5 minutes buffer
- 50 minutes (not 60) — leaves 10 minutes buffer
- Never 90 minutes unless it's a board meeting or strategic planning session

### Meeting decision tree
1. Does this meeting need to happen at all? → Could it be async (email, Loom, doc)?
2. Do I need to be in this meeting? → Could someone else represent me?
3. Does this need to be a group meeting? → Could it be 1:1 instead?
4. Is there an agenda? → If no agenda, decline the meeting.

### Meeting batching
- Cluster meetings on specific days (Tuesday/Thursday afternoons)
- Back-to-back meetings are better than scattered meetings (less context switching)
- Leave 10-minute gaps between meetings for notes and transitions

## Burnout prevention

### Warning signs
- Energy score below 5 for 2+ consecutive weeks
- Dreading Monday mornings
- Decision fatigue (avoiding decisions, delegating everything)
- Irritability with team
- Working weekends consistently
- No exercise or hobbies for 2+ weeks

### Recovery protocol

**Immediate (this week):**
- Cancel non-essential meetings
- Delegate 2-3 tasks that don't need you
- Take one afternoon completely off
- Exercise (even a 30-minute walk)

**Short-term (this month):**
- Take a 3-day weekend
- Review and reduce meeting load by 20%
- Add one more deep work block per week
- Revisit delegation triggers in ENERGY.md

**Medium-term (this quarter):**
- Take a full week off
- Hire or delegate to eliminate recurring energy drains
- Restructure the weekly schedule
- Consider whether the business model itself is draining (too many hats?)

## Delegation as energy management

The founder should only do work that meets ALL three criteria:
1. Only you can do it (no one else has the context or authority)
2. It's high-leverage (your involvement significantly changes the outcome)
3. It energizes you (or at minimum, doesn't drain you)

If a task fails any criterion, delegate it:
- Someone else can do it → delegate to team or OS framework
- It's low-leverage → automate or eliminate
- It drains you → find someone who's energized by this type of work

## Decision fatigue

Founders make hundreds of decisions daily. Quality degrades with volume.

**Reduce decision volume:**
- Set defaults for recurring decisions (this is what defaults.md is for)
- Delegate Type 2 decisions with clear guardrails
- Batch similar decisions (all hiring decisions on Thursday)
- Create policies for repeated situations

**Protect decision quality:**
- Make important decisions in the morning (peak cognitive energy)
- Never make Type 1 decisions when tired, hungry, or emotional
- Use the 24-hour rule for Type 1 decisions — sleep on it
- Review DECISIONS.md quarterly — are decision patterns improving?
